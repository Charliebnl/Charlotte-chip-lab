# How to Use CHIP

CHIP works best when you give it clear context.

---

## 🎯 Best input format

Send CHIP:

- your level
- the side you play
- your main struggle
- your goal
- optional clip or match context

---

## 🧪 Example prompts

### Build a plan

```text
Level: 2.5
Side: Right
Struggle: volleys + positioning
Goal: improve net play

Build me a 4-week improvement plan.
```
### Review a short clip
```text
I’m the player in black.
Level: 2.5
Side: Right
Please focus on volleys and recovery after volleys.

Review this short clip and tell me what to improve.
```

### Get one next focus

```text
Level: 3.0
Side: Left
I struggle with bandeja consistency.

What should I focus on in my next match?
```
---

## 📊 What CHIP returns

CHIP gives:

- a player snapshot
- key issues
- priority fixes (max 3)
- one next match focus
- a 4-week plan if requested

---

## ⚡️ Best practices

- Keep clips short (under 2 minutes)
- Always say which player you are
- Tell CHIP what you want feedback on
- Focus on one or two problems at a time

---

## ⚠️  What CHIP is not

CHIP is not:

- a biomechanics engine
- a full match analytics platform
- a replacement for a live coach

CHIP is a practical coaching agent designed to turn match input into useful next steps.
